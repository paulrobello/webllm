3. Surprise: this is not the GPU binding cap
--------------------------------------------
The §20-era hypothesis was that prefill-512 hits `maxStorageBufferBindingSize`
(128 MiB) directly. The captured signature contradicts that: the abort is in
ggml's *host-side* graph allocator (`ggml_tallocr_alloc` at ggml-alloc.c:82),
not the WebGPU device. The compute-graph backing buffer is sized too small —
only ~464 KiB free when a single ~7.64 MiB node-510 tensor wants to be placed.
Implication for Task 1:
  - The work is partly buffer *sizing* (grow the ggml compute-graph buffer to
    cover what tiled prefill actually needs), not only tile-size selection.
  - The 128 MiB GPU cap is still the upper bound on how large any one buffer
    can be bound, but well above the per-node need at prefill=512.


4. Per-node arithmetic
----------------------
Mistral-7B activation node at sequence length N:
  bytes = N × embeddingLength × sizeof(f32)
        = N × 4096 × 4
        = N × 16384

Verify the captured number (node_510 at seq=512):
  512 × 16384 = 8,388,608 bytes ≈ 8.0 MiB ≈ captured 8,011,776 bytes
  (The 4% delta is alignment / inner-dim shaving — matches "per-token f32
   activation" within rounding.)

Maximum tile that fits one bind slot (128 MiB cap):
  tileMax(no margin) = 134,217,728 / 16,384 = 8192 tokens
  tileMax(20% margin) = 8192 × 0.80      = 6553 tokens

Maximum tile that survives current graph-buffer slack (no Task-1 changes):
  tileFit(asis) = 475,648 / 16,384 ≈ 29 tokens   ← why prefill-512 aborts

Cumulative-graph projection (the §22 plan's `32 × N × 4096 × 4` form) is
the *worst-case sum across all 32 layers* if every layer's output tensor
co-resides in one binding (it does not, in llama.cpp's split-buffer
scheme — see ggml-backend split). Treat it as a soft upper bound:
  cumulative(N=512) = 32 × 512 × 4096 × 4 = 268,435,456 bytes (256 MiB)
  cumulative(N=128) = 32 × 128 × 4096 × 4 =  67,108,864 bytes ( 64 MiB)
  cumulative(N=256) = 32 × 256 × 4096 × 4 = 134,217,728 bytes (128 MiB)


5. Recommended Phase-1 tileSize
-------------------------------
**Recommendation: tileSize = 128**

Rationale:
  (a) Per-node at tile=128 → 128 × 4096 × 4 = 2,097,152 B (2 MiB) — comfortably
      under the 128 MiB binding cap (≈64× headroom).
  (b) Cumulative-graph projection at tile=128 → 64 MiB — exactly 50% of the
      128 MiB cap, leaving safe margin for KV-cache reads, weight rebinds,
      and intermediate workspace tensors that share bind slots.
  (c) Tile=128 is the largest power-of-two that keeps cumulative under
      cap × 0.5 — a robust default for the first tiling pass. We can revisit
      upward (256, 512) once Task 2 measures real per-tile timings.
  (d) Aligns with common k-quant block sizes (Q4_K block=256 elements, super-
      block multiple of 128) — avoids awkward partial-block tail handling.

Forward-looking ceiling (for Task 2/3 to validate empirically):
  tile=256 should fit (cumulative=128 MiB, exactly at cap — risky)
  tile=512 will not fit cumulative — cap exceeded by 2×
  Stay at tile=128 unless measurement says otherwise.

Caveat: Task 1 must *also* grow the ggml compute-graph buffer to accommodate
tile=128 worth of nodes. The current 475 KiB slack is the immediate blocker;
without resizing the graph buffer, even tile=32 would abort on layer 2+ of
a 32-layer prefill pass.
